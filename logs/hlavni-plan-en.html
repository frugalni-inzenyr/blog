System Definition: Kdo je Frugální Inženýr?

Člověk, který nahlíží na čas, peníze a životní rozhodnutí jako na systémy k optimalizaci:

  • > Frugalita: Odmítnutí statusového balastu, leasingového marketingu a předražených náplastí na zbytečnou práci. Maximalizace užitné hodnoty (Value for Money).
  • > Inženýrství: Stavění systémů odolných proti selhání (fault-tolerant), spoléhání se na tvrdá data, složené úročení a Monte Carlo simulace, nikoliv na pocity a náhodu.
  • > FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early): Finanční nezávislost a předčasný důchod. Stav, kdy pasivní příjmy z investovaného majetku spolehlivě pokryjí životní náklady a práce se stává čistě dobrovolnou volbou, nikoliv nutností k přežití.

System Definition: Who is the Frugal Engineer?

A systematic thinker who views time, money, and life decisions as code blocks to be refactored:

  • > Frugality: Rejecting status symbols, emotional spending, and leases. Maximizing utility and value for money while ignoring external vanity expectations.
  • > Engineering: Designing fault-tolerant structures, using data-driven pipelines, compound interest calculations, and Monte Carlo models rather than luck.
  • > FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early): A state where passive income generated by accumulated assets reliably covers all household expenses, turning employment from a survival necessity into a voluntary choice.

Master Plan: How the Frugal Engineer Designed His 50-Month Sprint to Financial Independence

[!NOTE] ⚡ TL;DR:

  • Goal: Reach Coast FIRE status within 50 months (by August 2030) and achieve absolute freedom of choice.
  • Strategy: Shifting from complex and high-risk real estate leverage to simplicity and peace of mind. Exit one property (a suburban apartment) and redeploy capital into a globally diversified ETF portfolio with a factor tilt.
  • Phases: A three-phase roadmap: starting with accumulation and cleaning up fraction assets, progressing through a property sale and deleveraging, and finishing with a passive ETF sprint leading to a long-term decision tree.

Just a few months ago, the Frugal Engineer had a completely different plan. He wanted to buy another investment property, use debt financing, and increase financial leverage. But the more he modeled his plan, the more he realized he was optimizing his net worth at the expense of his peace of mind.

In the end, he did the exact opposite:

  • Instead of a seventh apartment, he will sell one.
  • Instead of higher leverage, he wants lower debt.
  • Instead of a more complex system, he wants a simpler one.

This is his new Master Plan. Over the last 10 years, through a combination of a high savings rate, broad index investing, and active property management, he has built a solid foundation. This was the phase of raw, straightforward accumulation. Now, however, the family's financial system has reached a tipping point requiring optimization. The original concept of increasing debt leverage was evaluated through a risk audit and deemed too complex and stressful.

This 50-month sprint represents a safer, quieter path based on simplification, deleveraging, and maximizing the liquid ETF engine to eliminate Single Point of Failure (SPOF) in the family budget. The goal is no longer to generate the maximum amount of money, but to design a financial system that is good to live with and brings peace of mind.

Welcome to the system architecture of this optimization phase.

📊 Input Parameters (Baseline Config)

*Methodological note: All baseline inputs and ongoing contributions are stated in nominal terms (current prices of the respective year). Final target states and cyclic distributions are calculated in real terms (adjusted for inflation with a Czech target assumption of 2.0% p.a.) to preserve a realistic sense of purchasing power.*

The system initializes with the following configuration (values are fully adjusted for personal consumption and anonymized):

  • Net Worth (NW): Solid wealth foundation (the baseline starting point).
  • Asset Structure: Approximately one quarter in liquid investments (globally diversified ETF portfolio with a factor tilt + pension/DIP), the remainder in real estate (family home, mountain apartment, and investment apartments), cryptocurrencies, and receivables.
  • Debt Load: Mortgages with an overall LTV of around 60% (including unmortgaged properties, or 75% for mortgaged properties) with monthly payments serviced from regular surpluses.
  • Sanity Cushion: A cash reserve held throughout the sprint in a savings account to eliminate risks and ensure system stability.
  • Active Cash Flow: Consistent monthly surplus from active income (including rental income and repayments) routed directly into the investment pipeline.
  • Family Living Expenses: The planned household operating budget (Living OpEx – net family spending on food, utilities, kids, and operations, excluding mortgage payments which are modeled separately) is set at approx. 50,000 CZK monthly to serve as a baseline for modeling the target rent.

⚙️ The Investment Engine: Globally Diversified ETF Portfolio with a Factor Tilt

When allocating liquid assets, the Frugal Engineer focuses on low-cost passive index instruments. The core of the portfolio consists of a globally diversified equity index (tracking global markets), complemented by low-cost factor ETFs designed to tilt the portfolio toward value and small-cap stocks (Value and Small-cap premiums).

This asset allocation is designed to capture risk-factor premiums while preserving broad global diversification and leveraging the Czech 3-year capital gains tax exemption (0% income tax for physical persons).

🗺️ The Three-Phase 50-Month Roadmap

The plan is non-linear, divided into three distinct phases with clear objectives:

[Phase A: Months 1-9]     -->    [Phase B: Months 10-18]   -->    [Phase C: Months 19-50]
Accumulation & Clean-up          Property Exit (Suburban Apt)     Passive Sprint & Exit

Phase A: Cleaning Up Fraction Assets & Launching Accumulation (Months 1–9)

  • Objective: Simplify asset structures and establish a consistent investment pipeline.
  • Action: No additional real estate purchases, and no building savings (stavebko) drawdowns. Liquidate minor investment fractions and execute a one-time deployment of excess cash into the ETF portfolio. Channel monthly surpluses directly into ETFs. Notify the tenant of the property marked for sale that the lease will end on March 31, 2027.

Phase B: Suburban Apartment Exit & Deleveraging (Months 10–18)

  • Objective: Substantially reduce debt overhead (reducing investment and recreational properties from 6 to 5, and total property count from 7 to 6) and transition equity into liquid assets.
  • Action: In Month 10 (April 2027), sell the suburban apartment (at which point it meets the 5-year time test for acquisitions before 2021, and is therefore 100% tax-free). The entire released equity will be transferred into the ETF portfolio. Since the sold property is completely mortgage-free, there is no debt to settle, and the deployment will be made either as a lump-sum or gradually distributed over time to mitigate Sequence of Returns Risk. Monthly savings deposits drop due to the loss of the sold apartment's rental cash flow.

Phase C: The Passive Accumulation Sprint (Months 19–50)

  • Objective: Maximize the passive ETF base while leveraging professional career growth.
  • Action: Monthly surpluses are routed into the ETF engine. The second building savings account will remain undrawn (serviced as interest-only). Leverage increasing salary surpluses, concluding the active saving phase in August 2030.

📉 Target State at Month 50 (August 2030)

  • Personal ETF Portfolio: real (in today's purchasing power, adjusted for inflation) will reach a target value sufficient for a secure Coast FIRE status.
  • Liquid Reserve: Cash reserve in the savings account (fully preserved).
  • Total Net Worth (NW): real (in today's purchasing power, accounting for principal mortgage paydowns and a conservative real property growth of 2% p.a. above inflation) will increase.
  • Status: Secure Coast FIRE. The portfolio is large enough to compound on its own to carry the household to full retirement without further savings, while active work income covers current household living expenses.

🎛️ The Decision Tree (August 2034)

Once the 10-year tax-free tests on the remaining properties pass, the Frugal Engineer will choose between two exit paths:

  1. Complete Deleveraging (Conservative Security): Sell the investment apartments tax-free and pay off the remaining bank debt on the primary home and mountain apartment using ETF assets. The family becomes 100% debt-free with monthly payments dropping to zero and an ultra-safe SWR of under 2.00%.
  2. Hybrid Path (Rent Stabilization System): Sell only a portion of the investment apartments, pay down mortgage principal, retain the best rental units, and optimize participation in the pension and health insurance systems (e.g., via an s.r.o.) to act as a stabilizing element with an SWR of under 3.00%.

Note: Detailed mathematical models of both scenarios and optimization of pension and health system contributions will be analyzed in a separate future article.

🛡️ Disaster Recovery (Exception Handling)

  • 30% Market Corrections: In the active Coast FIRE accumulation phase, market corrections are ignored – the portfolio is not drawn down and continues to grow on paper, while living expenses are fully covered by active salary income. For the full retirement phase (Full FIRE) or in case of an unexpected loss of active income (Disaster Recovery), the system is shielded by dividing assets into three buckets (short-term cash, stabilizing assets, and long-term equities) to eliminate Sequence of Returns Risk. Layout validated by modeling historic market downturns.
  • Interest Rate Shocks: Mortgage refinance dates are staggered to avoid a single-year interest rate shock.
  • Mortality/Disability: Managed via the crisis plan (spouse as estate administrator, joint accounts, LLC routing, and term life insurance).

⚖️ Engineering Red-Teaming: The Clash of Three Worlds

This plan undergoes continuous internal scrutiny, balancing three competing life dynamics:

  1. The Real Estate Business (Leverage & Rent): Provides leverage but carries significant operational time and stress. For this reason, the decision was made not to buy any more properties or take out American home equity mortgages. Transitioning from complexity to simplicity is an investment in peace of mind.
  2. Passive Index Investing (ETFs): Ultimate liquidity, zero time overhead, global diversification. The final engine where capital from property exits is concentrated.
  3. Family Well-Being (Time and Freedom): The ultimate product of FIRE is not a large balance sheet, but owning one's time. Selling the suburban unit in Month 10 and capping properties at 5 is a direct step to free up mental bandwidth (slack space) for parenting and family harmony.

🏁 Conclusion

This plan is not a dogma, but a living architecture. Over the last ten years, the goal was to accumulate wealth as quickly as possible. For the next fifty months, the goal will be to build a financial system that will last for the next fifty years.

README.md

This project (the Frugal Engineer) documents the life and financial strategy of a technical professional (30+ years old, living in the Czech Republic) who views his professional, financial, and personal life as a system that can be analyzed, modeled, and optimized.

Over the last 10 years, through a combination of a high savings rate, investing in broad global ETFs, and managing real estate assets, he has built a solid financial foundation in the lower tens of millions of CZK. This was the phase of raw, straightforward accumulation and building system redundancy.

These logs are not a guide on how to get rich in a week. It is an engineering log and a system analysis of how a mind trained for extreme cost optimization behaves when it no longer needs to save. It is a search for the optimal operating point between financial independence and experiencing the present without constant Opportunity Cost calculations.

System Data (Frugal Setup):

Portfolio
 Globally diversified ETF portfolio (with a factor tilt) and rental properties (with an LTV of around 50%).
Fleet
 Korean family SUV (owned outright, free of leasing or debt).
Philosophy
 Time and absolute control over it have infinitely higher utility than status symbols and overpriced luxury.
Compiler
 Analytical concepts, data, and calculations are provided by the Frugal Engineer (human), while final text styling and generation are done with the help of AI (LLM).

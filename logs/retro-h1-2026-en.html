System Definition: Kdo je Frugální Inženýr?

Člověk, který nahlíží na čas, peníze a životní rozhodnutí jako na systémy k optimalizaci:

  • > Frugalita: Odmítnutí statusového balastu, leasingového marketingu a předražených náplastí na zbytečnou práci. Maximalizace užitné hodnoty (Value for Money).
  • > Inženýrství: Stavění systémů odolných proti selhání (fault-tolerant), spoléhání se na tvrdá data, složené úročení a Monte Carlo simulace, nikoliv na pocity a náhodu.
  • > FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early): Finanční nezávislost a předčasný důchod. Stav, kdy pasivní příjmy z investovaného majetku spolehlivě pokryjí životní náklady a práce se stává čistě dobrovolnou volbou, nikoliv nutností k přežití.

System Definition: Who is the Frugal Engineer?

A systematic thinker who views time, money, and life decisions as code blocks to be refactored:

  • > Frugality: Rejecting status symbols, emotional spending, and leases. Maximizing utility and value for money while ignoring external vanity expectations.

H1 2026 Retrospective: Refactoring the Family Financial Code

[!NOTE] ⚡ TL;DR:

  • Cycle Goal: Simplifying and refactoring the liquid portfolio – closing inefficient products (building savings and underperforming pension accounts) and consolidating capital into low-cost global ETFs.
  • Outcome (H1 2026): Investable Net Worth grew by +2.53%, global ETF positions were increased (by shifting released capital from closed building/pension savings), and real estate debt stabilized at a safe LTV of 49.19%.
  • System Lesson: Velocity of Capital is more critical than hoarding idle cash. Streamlining portfolio management reduces the cognitive overhead of the family system.

The completion of the first six months of 2026 offers an ideal opportunity to perform a routine retrospective and refactoring of the family financial system. In the spirit of agile development and an engineering approach, the balance sheet is evaluated not as a static number, but as a dynamic state diagram—a release that has undergone cleanup, dependency optimization, and the closure of inefficient branches (Single Points of Failure).

This document presents a structured retrospective for H1 2026 (comparing the state between January 2026 and June 2026). All absolute values and specific financial institution names are hidden to meet strict Stealth Wealth standards.

🎯 1. Cycle Goal (Sprint Goal)

The primary goal for the first half of 2026 was to clean up and simplify (refactor) the liquid portfolio. Specifically, this meant closing small, inefficient, or administratively heavy products and moving the released capital into low-cost global ETFs.

👍 3. What Went Well (What Worked)

  • Successful Broker Consolidation (ETF Accumulation):
    • An increase of +19.37 % in the primary accumulation account on the investment platform.
    • Setting up a new low-cost brokerage account at a foreign provider to receive the released liquidity. Direct global ETF purchases without intermediaries reduce the TCO of the entire portfolio.
  • Mortgage Refactoring and Collateral Release:
    • All mortgage loans were successfully consolidated and refinanced with a single bank.
    • This step allowed for releasing one of the investment apartments from bank collateral. The original intent was to prepare this property for potential re-mortgaging to fund further acquisitions. However, due to persistent high interest rates, this path is no longer economical. Nevertheless, releasing the apartment provides a major administrative simplification of the portfolio.
  • Liquidation of Inefficient Nodes & Savings Optimization:
    • Full closure of one building savings account (-100.00 %) was completed, transferring its funds to ETFs. The second building savings account was reduced to a minimum balance and reconfigured with a minimal amount as a flexible channel to ensure liquidity and speed of future withdrawal.
    • The pension savings account (-100.00 %), originally opened in 2020 primarily for employer contributions, was fully closed due to low performance, and the funds were moved directly to ETFs. Early termination involved a one-time tax penalty and returning state contributions, but this is an acceptable one-time cost for eliminating an inefficient and bureaucratic product.
  • Receivables Repayment (Liquidity Inflow):
    • The outstanding receivable from a side project (final settlement) was fully repaid (-100.00 %).
    • The outstanding intra-family receivable was partially repaid by -9.06 %. Both flows provided cash for immediate allocation.

👎 4. What Went Wrong / Obstacles (Bugs & Bottlenecks)

  • Crypto Write-offs (Bugs & Devaluation):
    • The altcoin exposure suffered a major blow—the position in the failed altcoin (PLU) approached zero and was fully written off (-100.00 %).
    • The primary cryptocurrency asset (Bitcoin) experienced a market valuation drop of -37.50 % (no sales took place, this represents paper volatility).
  • Refinancing Premium (Trade-off):
    • Consolidating all mortgages under a single bank came at a cost. Two properties originally had fixed mortgages from 2020 with interest rates around 2% p.a., which have now been replaced with current market rates. The increase in interest expenses is an acceptable trade-off for the gained flexibility (the released apartment).
  • Depreciation of Legacy Assets:
    • The second personal car was fully phased out of Net Worth tracking (-100.00 %), and the family SUV underwent a paper market value depreciation of -17.65 %.

📝 5. Lessons Learned

  1. Administrative Overhead is a Hidden Cost (TCO): Having small, scattered amounts across multiple platforms (real estate crowdfunding, secondary brokers, building savings) generates a cognitive load that does not match the returns. Consolidating into global ETFs and under a single mortgage provider has dramatically freed up cognitive capacity.
  2. Collateral Flexibility Takes Precedence Over the Lowest Interest Rate: Losing the ultra-low 2% rates on two 2020 mortgages hurts, but the ability to release an apartment and simplify future real estate transactions gives the portfolio strategic maneuverability.

  • [ ] Keep total mortgage portfolio LTV stabilized around 50%.

README.md

This project (the Frugal Engineer) documents the life and financial strategy of a technical professional (30+ years old, living in the Czech Republic) who views his professional, financial, and personal life as a system that can be analyzed, modeled, and optimized.

Over the last 10 years, through a combination of a high savings rate, investing in broad global ETFs, and managing real estate assets, he has built a solid financial foundation in the lower tens of millions of CZK. This was the phase of raw, straightforward accumulation and building system redundancy.

These logs are not a guide on how to get rich in a week. It is an engineering log and a system analysis of how a mind trained for extreme cost optimization behaves when it no longer needs to save. It is a search for the optimal operating point between financial independence and experiencing the present without constant Opportunity Cost calculations.

System Data (Frugal Setup):

Portfolio
 Globally diversified ETF portfolio (with a factor tilt) and rental properties (with an LTV of around 50%).
Fleet
 Korean family SUV (owned outright, free of leasing or debt).
Philosophy
 Time and absolute control over it have infinitely higher utility than status symbols and overpriced luxury.
Compiler
 Analytical concepts, data, and calculations are provided by the Frugal Engineer (human), while final text styling and generation are done with the help of AI (LLM).
